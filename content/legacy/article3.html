5 Tips to Maximize Your Earnings as a TheRain Driver in Cameroon

May 20, 2026
Cameroon
7 min read
Drivers who earn the most on TheRain follow a few practical habits. Better routes, smarter hours, and a high trust score all add up. Here are five things top drivers do consistently.
1
Drive During Peak Hours
The highest demand periods in Cameroon are morning rush hours (6AM–9AM), lunch breaks (12PM–2PM), and evening rush hours (5PM–9PM). During these times, ride requests surge and you can complete more trips in less time.
Pro Tip: Set your schedule around these peak hours to maximize your daily earnings.
2
Use the Heatmap Feature
TheRain's heatmap shows you exactly where demand is highest in real-time. Red zones mean high demand — position yourself there before accepting rides. This reduces your wait time between trips and increases your hourly earnings significantly.
High demand zones in Douala: Akwa, Bonanjo, Bonapriso. In Yaoundé: Centre-ville, Bastos, Nlongkak.
3
Maintain a High Trust Score
Your Trust Score directly affects how often you receive ride requests. Drivers with higher trust scores get priority dispatch — meaning you get matched with riders first. Keep your score high by being professional, punctual, and courteous.

4
Accept School Transport Routes
School transport routes offer guaranteed income with predictable schedules. Morning and afternoon school runs provide consistent earnings even during low-demand periods. Drivers who take on school routes report 30-40% higher monthly earnings compared to ride-only drivers.
School routes run Monday–Friday, giving you a reliable income base to build on.
5
Keep Your Vehicle in Top Condition
A clean, well-maintained vehicle leads to better rider ratings, which boosts your Trust Score, which leads to more ride requests. Respond to AI maintenance alerts promptly — ignoring them can lead to breakdowns that cost you more in lost earnings than the repair itself.
TheRain's AI predicts maintenance needs before they become problems — oil changes, brake wear, tire replacement, and battery issues.
Average Driver Earnings
Top TheRain drivers in Douala and Yaoundé earn between 150,000 – 300,000 XAF per month working full-time. Part-time drivers working peak hours earn 60,000 – 120,000 XAF per month. School transport drivers add an additional 40,000 – 80,000 XAF per month.
